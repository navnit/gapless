How it works

Open. Tune. Export.

  1. 01 · Open

    Drop in a local video. There's one screen and no timeline to build. Your project autosaves as you work, and your original file stays read-only.

  2. 02 · Tune

    Drag the threshold; every proposed cut updates live on the timeline. Click any hatched cut to keep that section. Your original file is never modified.

  3. 03 · Export

    Render a finished MP4 that matches exactly what the Edited preview showed. There's nothing else to configure.

Use cases

Silence hides everywhere.

01 · Meetings & demos

The demo worked. The waiting didn't.

Screen shares booting, apps processing, "can everyone see my screen?" Recorded meetings are full of dead stretches. Gapless finds each quiet one and closes it, so the recording you share is just the demo.

02 · Screencasts & tutorials

Narrate at your own pace.

Pause between sentences. Check your notes. Re-find your cursor. Record however you naturally would, then set the threshold, and everything quieter than it gets trimmed. A margin stays around every cut so the edit still breathes.

03 · Podcasts & interviews

Keep the conversation. Lose the lag.

Guests think before they answer, sometimes for a while. Gapless tightens the long pauses to a natural beat without touching a single word, and it leaves a sliver of silence on each side so nothing sounds clipped.

04 · Lectures & talks

Don't cut. Fast-forward.

Sometimes the silence is the content: someone writing on a whiteboard, a class working through a problem. Switch from cutting to fast-forwarding and those quiet stretches play at 8× instead of vanishing. Nothing is lost, it's just compressed.

Why Gapless exists

I was demoing our product on a call. Every time I clicked Run, everyone sat and watched a progress bar crawl. The recording came out 22 minutes long, for maybe nine minutes of actual demo. Cutting the waiting out by hand took longer than the meeting had.

Gapless is the tool I wanted that afternoon: open the recording, review the cuts, keep the nine minutes that matter. It's free, open-source, and built as the visual interface for the excellent auto-editor engine.

FAQ

Questions, answered.

Is Gapless free?

Yes. Gapless is free and open-source under the GPL-3.0 license. There is no account to create, no watermark on your exports, and no paid tier.

Does Gapless upload my videos to a server?

No. Everything runs locally on your own machine. Your files never leave your device and no network connection is required, even for the first launch.

How does Gapless remove silence from a video?

Gapless scans the audio and proposes a cut wherever the sound drops below a threshold you set. Every proposed cut appears on a visual timeline, so you can review them, keep any you want, and export a tighter MP4.

How is Gapless different from auto-editor?

Gapless is the visual interface for the auto-editor engine, which it bundles in, version-pinned and checksum-verified. You get a timeline to review and adjust every cut, with no Python to install and no command line to learn.

Does Gapless change my original file?

No. The edit is completely non-destructive. Your original file stays read-only and Gapless writes the result to a separate MP4 that matches exactly what the Edited preview showed.

What operating systems does Gapless support?

Gapless runs on macOS 12 or later, on both Apple Silicon and Intel Macs. Windows and Linux versions are coming soon.

Why does macOS say Gapless can't be opened or might harm my Mac?

Because Gapless is not yet notarized by Apple. Notarizing an app, and the Developer ID certificate it requires, means enrolling in Apple's Developer Program at $99 a year, and Gapless is a free open-source project. The app is safe: every line of its source is public and each DMG is SHA-256 verified. On first launch, approve it once via System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway. Apps installed with Homebrew open without any prompt.

Can I keep pauses I want instead of cutting everything?

Yes. Click any proposed cut to restore that section, adjust the margin kept on both sides of every cut, or switch from cutting to fast-forwarding, which plays silent stretches at 8× instead of removing them.
